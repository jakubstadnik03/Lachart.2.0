I'm Jakub Stadnik — and I'm reaching out because you run a place where lactate testing is taken seriously. That's exactly who we built LaChart for.

LaChart started because as a racer and a coach myself I could never accept that lactate values, FTP and training zones end up living in a spreadsheet or a paper folder. This is the tool where a testing center and a coach run the entire athlete record — from a single finger-prick on the bench to a full season plan.

What LaChart does

From raw stages to a finished report in minutes.

You enter the stages of a test — power or pace, heart rate, lactate — and LaChart fits multiple models in parallel. You pick the one that matches your protocol, or move the thresholds by hand.

Curve & thresholds
Test analysis

LT1 and LT2 from multiple methods at once

Dmax, log-log, fixed 2.0 and 4.0 mmol/L, OBLA 2.0–3.5, LTP1/LTP2. See them all side by side, pick the method that matches your protocol — zones recompute instantly.

  • Multiple threshold methods — choose the one you trust
  • Manual override — drag a point on the curve or type a value
  • Compare against the last test right in the chart
Your branding
PDF report

A report you'll be happy to hand over

Clean layout, readable typography, stage-by-stage table, curve with thresholds and your zones drawn in. Your testing center's logo and colors in the header — the athlete gets a document worth what they paid for the test.

  • Custom logo and colors in the header
  • Athlete and coach versions in one click
  • Stage-by-stage table with lactate, power and heart rate
Training zones

Zones tailored to the athlete, not a formula

5, 7, or your own split. LaChart keeps separate values for power, pace and heart rate — so running, cycling and swimming each get their own zones, done right.

  • Cycling, running, swimming — each sport has its own zones
  • Change history — see how an athlete's zones have moved
  • Export to TrainingPeaks, Garmin, Wahoo or PDF
Who it's built for

Built for testing centers and coaches — not a hobbyist app.

LaChart covers both roles at once. The testing center keeps the database of athletes and completed tests; the coach takes that data straight into a training plan. When an athlete works with both, they see each other's data — without anyone emailing PDFs around.

For testing centers and labs
